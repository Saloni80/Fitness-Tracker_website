Proper sleep is essential for physical health, emotional well-being, and cognitive function. It involves getting enough restful, uninterrupted sleep—typically 7 to 9 hours per night for most adults.

Quality sleep supports immune function, memory consolidation, mood regulation, and recovery of the body and brain. To achieve proper sleep, it’s important to maintain a consistent sleep schedule, create a relaxing bedtime routine, limit exposure to screens and caffeine before bed, and ensure the sleep environment is quiet, dark, and cool.

Chronic lack of sleep can lead to fatigue, weakened immunity, poor concentration, and increased risk of chronic illnesses like heart disease and diabetes.
